Scope by project phase

From first cut to finish grade.

The work is grouped by what the property needs next, not by a generic list of machine hours.

Access and clearing

Make room without losing the shape of the property.

Selective or full clearing, brush removal, stump work, debris handling, demolition, and temporary construction access are scoped around the buildable area and the route equipment can safely use.

  • Land clearing to an agreed limit
  • Stump grinding or removal options
  • Demolition and debris removal
  • Temporary access and laydown area

Cut, fill, and site preparation

Establish the working elevations before the finish pass.

Building pad rough-out, cut and fill, compaction passes, finish grading, and driveway shaping are coordinated to the plan when one exists and to practical site conditions when it does not.

  • Building pad rough-out
  • Rough and finish grading
  • Driveway and road-base shaping
  • Steep cuts flagged for engineering input

Drainage and water control

Give water a deliberate route across the property.

Ponds, swales, French drains, culverts, spillways, and erosion controls are planned around the same grade that carries vehicles and supports the build.

  • Pond excavation and bank shaping
  • Swales, drains, culverts, and outlets
  • Spillway and overflow grading
  • Erosion and sediment controls

Finish and closeout

Walk the grade, check the drainage, leave the site legible.

The last pass is about function: positive drainage, clean transitions, usable access, managed debris, and a walkthrough of what was completed and what still depends on the next trade.

  • Finish grading and transition cleanup
  • Drainage path review
  • Debris and disturbed-area check
  • Owner walkthrough and handoff

How a job moves

A visible sequence from site walk to closeout.

No invented day counts. Terrain, access, weather, permitting, and the final scope determine the schedule.

  1. 01

    Read

    Walk the property

    Review access, boundaries, timber, rock, wet ground, existing drainage, and what the site needs to support next.

  2. 02

    Define

    Set the scope

    Agree on clearing limits, debris handling, cut and fill intent, access, drainage work, and any permit or locate dependencies.

  3. 03

    Open

    Clear and establish access

    Open the working area and build the route machines and material need to move through the site.

  4. 04

    Shape

    Cut, fill, and drain

    Establish rough grade, work the drainage path, shape pads and drives, and prepare for the finish pass.

  5. 05

    Close

    Finish and walk through

    Review the final grade, water movement, access, cleanup, and the handoff to the owner or next trade.

Fleet logic

The right machine for the ground in front of it.

Class-level roles only. Exact equipment varies by job, access, and the work being performed.

Primary grade machine

Grading dozer

Rough cut, fill placement, clearing push-outs, pad shaping, and the long controlled passes that make grade read as one surface.

Reach and excavation

Excavator

Stumps, trenching, ponds, drainage runs, demolition, and work that needs reach more than a broad grading pass.

Tight-access support

Skid steer

Close work, cleanup, material handling, and grading where a larger machine does not have room to operate.

Material movement

Haul truck

Debris, fill, stone, and material movement on or off the property as the site plan requires.

    Before the site walk

    Questions worth resolving early.

    Property-specific requirements still need to be confirmed for the actual parcel and scope.

    Do I need a permit to clear or grade?

    Requirements depend on the county, parcel, disturbed area, nearby streams or wetlands, and the type of work. The site walk identifies which questions need to be confirmed before equipment moves.

    Do you grind stumps or remove them?

    Both can be considered. The right approach depends on what will be built, whether the ground must be excavated later, and how debris will be handled.

    What happens if the ground is saturated?

    Wet ground can change access, compaction, erosion control, and the sequence of work. Conditions are reviewed against the actual site instead of forcing a generic schedule.

    Can clearing, grading, and driveway work be combined?

    They can be scoped together when the property and construction sequence support it. The site walk helps define the shared access, material movement, drainage, and finish requirements.

    How is the final price determined?

    Pricing is based on the actual scope, terrain, access, debris handling, material movement, drainage work, and dependencies identified for the property. The planning tool above does not calculate price.

    What should I have ready for the first visit?

    Property access, parcel or survey information if available, known utility locations, the intended build area, drainage concerns, and any photos or plans that clarify what comes next.
