01 The vehicle

Meridian

Two stages. A booster that comes home. One engine family, flown from sea level to vacuum.

Meridian is a two-stage orbital launch vehicle. Its first stage is designed to fly, return, and fly again. Its second stage is built around a single restartable engine. Both stages burn the same propellant, lit by the same engine family: Ardent.

03 Destinations

Where Meridian delivers

Meridian is tuned for the orbits where most payloads actually go: low, fast, and Sun-synchronous.

LEOApprox. 200-1,000 km

Low Earth Orbit

Constellations, technology demonstration, and resupply. The busiest neighbourhood in space, and the one Meridian is built for.

SSOApprox. 600-800 km

Sun-Synchronous Orbit

Earth observation and remote sensing, crossing each point at the same local sun-time. Polar, exacting, and unforgiving on insertion accuracy.

INCOn request

Dedicated inclination

Your orbit, your inclination, your timing. A dedicated mission places the payload without a rideshare compromise on destination.

DedicatedThe whole vehicle, your orbit. Full control of inclination, altitude, and window.

RideshareShare the ride to a standard orbit on a fixed cadence. Lower cost, set destination.

04 Operations

How a mission flies

From signed manifest to separation confirmation, a Meridian mission moves through the same disciplined sequence every time.

  1. 01

    Manifest

    Lock mass, volume, orbit, and window. Assign a mission manager through flight.

  2. 02

    Integration

    Receive, inspect, and integrate the spacecraft to the payload adapter.

  3. 03

    Encapsulation

    Seal the payload inside the fairing under cleanroom conditions.

  4. 04

    Rehearsal

    Run propellant load, countdown, and hold before committing to flight.

  5. 05

    Launch

    The window opens. The count runs. Nine engines light.

  6. 06

    Insertion

    Stage two sets the orbit, confirms the state vector, and deploys.
