01The vehicle

Meridian

Two stages. A booster that comes home. One engine family, flown from sea level to vacuum.

Meridian is a two-stage orbital launch vehicle. Its first stage is designed to fly, return, and fly again. Its second stage is built around a single restartable engine — precise enough to set a payload in its slot and walk away. Both stages burn the same propellant, lit by the same engine family: Ardent.

01Propulsion

Nine Ardent engines

A sea-level cluster, deep-throttle capable. Eight hold the ring; one gimbals at the centre for control through ascent and landing.

02Stage one

The booster that returns

After separation the first stage reorients, relights, and flies a controlled descent to be recovered and reflown. Reuse is the architecture, not an afterthought.

03Staging

Clean separation

Pneumatic pushers and a vented interstage part the stages without pyrotechnic shock reaching the payload.

04Stage two

One engine, vacuum-tuned

A single restartable Ardent Vacuum carries the stack to orbit, then relights to circularise, phase, and deorbit itself — leaving nothing behind.

03Destinations

Where Meridian delivers

Meridian is tuned for the orbits where most of the world's payloads actually go — low, fast, and Sun-synchronous.

LEO≈ 200–1,000 km

Low Earth Orbit

Constellations, technology demonstration, resupply. The busiest neighbourhood in space — and the one Meridian is built for.

SSO≈ 600–800 km

Sun-Synchronous Orbit

Earth observation and remote sensing, crossing each point at the same local sun-time. Polar, exacting, unforgiving on insertion accuracy.

04Operations

How a mission flies

From signed manifest to separation confirmation, a Meridian mission moves through the same disciplined sequence every time. Predictability is the product.

  1. 01

    Manifest

    We lock your mass, volume, orbit, and window, and assign a mission manager who stays with you to flight.

  2. 02

    Integration

    Your spacecraft is received, inspected, and integrated to the payload adapter in a controlled facility.

  3. 03

    Encapsulation

    Sealed inside the fairing under cleanroom conditions, then mated to the vehicle.

  4. 04

    Rehearsal

    A full wet dress rehearsal — propellant load, countdown, and hold — before anyone commits to flight.

  5. 05

    Launch

    The window opens. The count runs. Nine engines light and the stack leaves the pad.

  6. 06

    Insertion

    Stage two sets you in your slot, confirms the state vector, and deploys. You have telemetry before the booster is home.
