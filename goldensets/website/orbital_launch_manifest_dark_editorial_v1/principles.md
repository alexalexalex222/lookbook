# Orbital Launch Manifest Dark Editorial — Anchor Principles

- The first viewport pairs one large promise with a technical launch-pad elevation; the vehicle is the visual evidence, not atmospheric stock imagery.
- Vehicle anatomy is selectable and labeled by assembly so users can inspect propulsion, stages, staging, and payload relationships.
- Narrative order is fixed: threshold, vehicle, envelope, orbit, mission sequence, roadmap, manifest intake.
- Specs use constrained labels, tables, and readouts. Unsupplied figures remain placeholders or disappear.
- Orbit graphics and mission timelines explain operational relationships; they do not imply proven capability.
- Responsive behavior collapses complex grids into single-column modules while preserving labels, diagram context, form review, and the dark technical atmosphere.
