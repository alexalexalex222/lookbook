# Iron Circuit Fight Academy - Design Principles

This is the professional combat-sports anchor. Its job is to make a first visit
feel understandable while preserving the authority of a real training room.

## Composition

- The first viewport is a full-bleed training-floor scene, not a card collage.
- The scene earns realism through equipment, depth, material wear, floor
  geometry, and light. A few simple fighter silhouettes are not a substitute.
- The primary split is beginner foundations versus intermediate fight lab.
  Neither path is treated as a lesser version of the other.
- Information alternates between immersive scenes and hard, scannable
  instruments: tabs, schedule, timeline, role ledger, plan, checklist, form.
- Section alignment is deliberately left-led. Centering is reserved for compact
  states, not the whole page.

## Visual System

- Neutral ramp: chalk and steel through deep charcoal and black.
- Accent discipline: red marks action and intensity; blue marks training lanes
  and secondary structure. Neither becomes a page-wide wash.
- Display type is condensed and athletic. Body copy remains calm and readable.
- Corners stay sharp or lightly rounded. Elevation is restrained and physical.
- Motion feels like equipment feedback: short, controlled, and easy to disable.

## Interaction

- Program and schedule controls use real tab semantics and keyboard navigation.
- Mobile navigation traps no one: focus is visible, Escape closes it, and links
  return the page to a stable state.
- Form errors are specific, preserve input, and move focus to the first problem.
- Success states explain that the static donor did not transmit information.

## Proof Discipline

Trust comes from the clarity of the room, the training path, and the visit flow.
Do not replace missing evidence with belts, records, named coaches, ratings,
member counts, or vague superlatives. Proof-safe role labels are stronger than
beautiful fabricated biographies.

## Routing Boundary

Route here for professional martial arts, MMA, boxing, Muay Thai, BJJ,
wrestling, striking, grappling, fight academies, trial-class flows, and
beginner-to-intermediate training systems. Do not route here for wellness,
family fitness, kids-only karate, generic gyms, or broadcast/event promotion.
