Role-based views

Three roles, three scoped surfaces.

Permissions are a short, readable list per role — not an inherited pile of flags nobody can reconstruct later.

Operator

Works the queue day to day — claims, resolves, and hands off signals as they come in.

  • Claim, snooze, and comment on signals in their team's queues
  • Reassign within their team's roster
  • Cannot edit alert rules or thresholds
  • Cannot see queues outside assigned teams

Lead

Owns the rules and the escalation path — sets the conditions operators work against.

  • Everything an operator can, across every team
  • Create, edit, and disable alert rules
  • Cannot change billing or workspace-level SSO

Auditor

Reads everything, changes nothing — the role that makes the audit trail worth having.

  • View every queue and rule, across every team
  • Search and export the full audit trail
  • Cannot claim, snooze, reassign, or edit a rule

Workflow

From raw stream to resolved, on one screen.

The path is deliberately short: connect a source, shape it into a queue, and hand the team a view they can act on.

  1. 01
    Connect

    Point a source at SignalStack — webhook, log drain, or polling job. Events land in seconds.

    webhook · log drain · polling
  2. 02
    Shape

    Map fields to a queue, set status states, and define the thresholds that matter to you.

    fields → queue → thresholds
  3. 03
    Operate

    The team works the queue. Alerts fire, ownership is clear, and the audit trail writes itself.

    claim · snooze · escalate
  4. 04
    Review

    Leads tune thresholds against what the audit trail actually showed. Auditors confirm nothing slipped through unlogged.

    audit trail → rule tuning

For the terminal, not just the browser

Every click above maps to a documented call.

The console is a convenience layer. Claim a signal, list a queue, export the log — automate any of it from a script instead of a keyboard.

REST — list open signals 
curl -H "Authorization: Bearer [API_KEY]" \
  https://api.signalstack.example/v1/queues/incident-triage/signals?status=at_risk
CLI — claim a signal 
# requires the signalstack CLI, authenticated once per machine
signalstack queue claim --id [SIGNAL_ID] --queue incident-triage
REST — export the audit trail 
curl -H "Authorization: Bearer [API_KEY]" \
  https://api.signalstack.example/v1/audit?since=[ISO_TIMESTAMP] -o audit.csv

Rate limits, pagination, and webhook payload shapes live in the published API reference — this is the shape of it, not the whole document.
