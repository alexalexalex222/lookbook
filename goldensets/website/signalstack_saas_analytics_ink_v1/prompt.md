# SignalStack — Route Prompt

Use for incident operations, alert ownership, triage queue, escalation, and operational workflow interfaces.

It can also support a B2B analytics or admin-console landing page when the first viewport still leads with a real, dense product surface rather than invented KPI marketing.

The first screen is the product: a dense owned-alert queue with filters, status summary, selected-signal detail, owner and source context, action feedback, and incident timeline. Continue into workflow stages, permission boundaries, loading/empty/error/success states, auditability, deployment options, and a workflow-mapping intake.

Favor neutral ink surfaces, restrained blue signal color, compact controls, readable status chips, and table-first hierarchy. Marketing copy stays subordinate to the operating surface.

Do not invent customer logos, integrations, uptime, SLA, incident counts, resolution speed, prices, or security/compliance claims.
