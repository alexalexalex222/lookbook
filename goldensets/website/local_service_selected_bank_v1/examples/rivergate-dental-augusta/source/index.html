Common Questions

Before your
first visit.

I haven’t seen a dentist in years. Will I be lectured?

No. Most weeks we welcome someone who has been away a decade or more. Your first visit is an unhurried exam and a conversation about where you would like to be — nothing happens without your say-so.

What happens at a first appointment?

Plan on about ninety minutes: a comprehensive exam, 3D imaging, a gum-health check, and time with your doctor to review findings and options. Cleanings are usually scheduled once we know what your mouth needs.

Do you file dental insurance?

We file claims with most major PPO plans on your behalf and provide written estimates before treatment begins. For patients without insurance, we offer a simple in-house membership that covers routine care.

Is sedation right for me?

IV and oral sedation are options for anxious patients and longer procedures. Your doctor reviews your health history and current medications first, then walks you through exactly what to expect. Sedation is only recommended when it is appropriate for you.

How long does implant treatment take?

Every case differs, but implant treatment typically spans a few months: placement, a healing period while the implant integrates, then the final crown. You receive a written timeline at your consultation.
