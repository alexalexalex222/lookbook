Straight Answers

Commission questions, answered.

What does a new fence cost?

Fencing is priced by the linear foot and varies with material, terrain, and gate work — cedar, steel, vinyl, and aluminum each carry different rates. After a site walk you receive a written, fixed quote; the number we sign is the number you pay.

How long does installation take?

Most residential builds are completed in two to four working days once permits and HOA approval are in hand. Scheduling typically runs two to three weeks out; we confirm your dates in writing before we break ground.

Do you handle HOA approvals and permits?

Yes. We prepare the drawings, submit to your HOA and municipality, and schedule utility marking before any post hole is dug. It is part of the commission, not an upcharge.

What about property lines?

We build from your recorded survey or plat. If you do not have one, we can coordinate a licensed surveyor before layout — a fence set on the wrong line is a problem we refuse to build.

What maintenance will my fence need?

Cedar benefits from re-sealing every two to three years; ornamental steel and aluminum need little beyond an occasional wash; vinyl asks only for a rinse. We leave every client a one-page care sheet for their material.

Is the work under warranty?

Every build carries our five-year workmanship warranty on posts, rails, and gates, alongside the manufacturer warranties on material. If a gate sags or a post moves, we come back and make it right.
