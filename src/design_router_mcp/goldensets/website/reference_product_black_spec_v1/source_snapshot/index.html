No. 02 The idea

A stopped watch is right twice a day. Nadir is built to never be stopped, or wrong.

Every choice inside Nadir — the free-sprung balance, the mass of the case, the geometry of the lever — exists to hold a rate, not to announce one. There is no complication here that is not the hour.

What is left is a small, exact discipline: a watch that agrees with itself, day after day, whether or not anyone is checking. We check anyway. That is the job.

No. 03 The object

Nothing on this case is only for looking at.

Every visible surface either does mechanical work, protects something that does, or makes the time easier to read. The watch can be understood in profile before the case back is opened.

  1. 01

    Machined, then made quiet

    The grade-5 titanium case is bead-blasted across its broad surfaces. A narrow polished edge separates the planes without turning the case into a mirror.

  2. 02

    A crystal shaped for a glance

    The domed sapphire is box-ground and anti-reflective on its underside only, preserving the hard outer surface.

  3. 03

    Legible before beautiful

    Applied indices and faceted hands catch light at arm's length. The central seconds hand is the only warm mark on the dial.

  4. 04

    The crown belongs to the case

    Its knurl follows the case finish, the collar is sealed, and the signed cap remains visible only when the wrist turns.

  5. 05

    The strap begins inside the lug

    Quick-release hardware sits between drilled lugs so the attachment reads as structure rather than an afterthought.

No. 04 The motion

The part you can hear is the least of it.

A movement does not fail loudly. It drifts — a half-second here, a degree of amplitude there — long before it is noticeable by ear. The work is in keeping the swing consistent as the mainspring releases.

Isochronism is the promise that today's second is the same length as yesterday's.

No. 05 The build

Five stages. One person carries the watch through all of them.

A single build runs from billet to signed timing card before the next one begins.

  1. 01

    Billet

    A single block of grade-5 titanium is cut for the case — not cast, not stamped.

  2. 02

    Movement

    The calibre is assembled dry, oiled by hand, and run until it settles into its own rate.

  3. 03

    Regulation

    One watchmaker adjusts the same unit across positions until the rate agrees with itself everywhere.

  4. 04

    Casing

    Movement, dial, and hands are cased under filtered air, then pressure-tested with the crown sealed.

  5. 05

    Certification

    The finished watch is timed again and the person who regulated it signs the card that ships inside the case.

No. 09 Questions

The things people ask before they reserve.

No fine print buried three clicks away. If it matters before reserving, it belongs here.

How long does regulation take?

Each movement is adjusted across positions until the rate holds — sometimes a week, sometimes longer. Certification happens when the unit is true, not when the calendar says to.

Can I choose the strap?

Yes. Every Nadir ships on the quick-release strap chosen at reservation, with a second strap included.

What if it is not the watch I thought it was?

Wear it during the 14-day home trial. If it is not right, return it for a full refund with no restocking fee.

How is it serviced?

Every Nadir is built to be opened again. It returns to the workshop for service and regulation.

What happens after I reserve?

Your place is held in the current build queue. The workshop writes when the unit is cased, regulated, certified, and ready to ship.
