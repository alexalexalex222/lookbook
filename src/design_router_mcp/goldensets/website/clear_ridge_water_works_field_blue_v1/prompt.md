# Clear Ridge Water Works - Route Prompt

Build a premium rural-water field-service website around one sequence: read the
symptom, trace the system, confirm the property, and stage a useful service
request. The page should feel like a calm technician's field notebook rather
than a billboard or a generic plumbing template.

Use mineral green, water teal, limestone, graphite, brass, and restrained clay
orange. Pair editorial serif headings with practical body copy and monospaced
instrument labels. Detailed inline SVG should expose real system relationships:
geology, well casing, drop pipe, pump, pressure tank, switch, filters, valves,
gauges, bypasses, service drains, property routes, and field notes.

Build the complete flow:

1. Full-width hero with a private-system cutaway and urgent-request affordance.
2. Accessible symptom workbench for no water, low pressure, cloudy water,
   cycling, taste or odor, and filtration.
3. A validated service request that inherits selected symptom and urgency.
4. Pressure-cycle and filtration-serviceability field studies.
5. A six-point system verification sequence.
6. Honest urgent versus planned service paths.
7. Property-specific coverage flow with a conceptual terrain route.
8. Asymmetric maintenance-scope comparison.
9. Proof-safe service-record structure.
10. FAQ, closing action, and complete footer.

Interactions must carry meaning: roving-focus symptom tabs, symptom-to-form
handoff, urgency selection, coverage focus, maintenance-scope handoff,
focus-managed validation, mobile menu trapping, and reduced-motion fallbacks.

Use system fonts, semantic HTML, CSS, detailed inline SVG, and minimal vanilla
JavaScript. No external assets, stock trucks, repeated flat house icons, emoji
icons, or raw horizontal mobile scrollers.

Proof policy: invent no licenses, insurance, certifications, response times,
guarantees, towns, coverage, reviews, measurements, customer outcomes, prices,
or service history. A conceptual diagram is not repair advice, and a property
route is not a coverage promise.
