From aquifer to tap

One system. Six places to verify.

Private water systems are connected. A pressure complaint can begin at the filter, tank, switch, check valve or pump, so the field visit follows the route instead of replacing parts by hunch.

  1. 01
    Wellhead and casing

    Cap, wiring route, sanitary seal and visible condition.

  2. 02
    Pump and water column

    Electrical draw, delivery behavior and recovery under use.

  3. 03
    Check valve and line

    Whether the supply holds pressure between cycles.

  4. 04
    Tank and controls

    Air charge, drawdown, gauge response and switch condition.

  5. 05
    Filtration stages

    Loading, pressure drop, bypass position and service access.

  6. 06
    House supply

    Delivered flow, pressure and the final route to fixtures.

Before the visit

Straight answers about rural water service.

Request service
Can you tell what is wrong over the phone?

We can narrow the likely area and decide which service path fits. The on-site test confirms the cause before work is scoped.

What counts as urgent?

No water to the home, an active leak, or a pump that will not run. Mark the request urgent and current availability will be confirmed directly.

Do you work on systems you did not install?

Yes. Rural systems often combine equipment added over time, so the first visit maps what is actually installed.

What is short-cycling?

It is rapid pump switching instead of a normal run length. It often points to pressure storage, controls, a check valve or a leak and can add wear to the pump.

Do I need a water test before asking about filtration?

Bring any recent test you have. If you do not have one, describe what you want to change and the existing system can be reviewed before treatment is scoped.

How is coverage confirmed?

Send a road, general area or county with the system type and timing. Coverage and current availability are checked before scheduling.
