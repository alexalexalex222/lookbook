# Clear Ridge Water Works - Design Principles

This is the diagnostic rural-water anchor. Its strongest idea is that system
literacy comes before sales.

## Composition

- The hero combines direct symptom language with an inspectable well-to-home
  cutaway. It does not lead with company praise.
- The symptom workbench is the primary decision instrument. It teaches where a
  problem may sit, then carries that choice into the request.
- Technical studies use field-sheet structure, numbered callouts, sectional
  views, real component relationships, and material detail.
- Light limestone surfaces orient the user. Mineral-green instruments hold the
  denser technical work. Clay orange marks action and urgency.
- Coverage is framed as a property review, not a decorative map promise.

## Visual System

- Neutral ramp: paper white, limestone, weathered stone, graphite, near-black.
- Brand ramp: mineral green and water teal, with restrained clay-orange signal.
- Serif display type adds documentary authority; body and mono labels keep the
  page practical.
- Corners are small. Panels read like field plates, not soft SaaS cards.
- One scene, one job: each SVG explains a distinct system or property question.

## Interaction

- Symptom tabs implement roving focus and paired tab panels.
- Diagnostic and service-path actions prefill or focus the relevant form field.
- Form errors are specific, preserve input, and focus the first invalid field.
- Static success states say that no data was sent.
- Native FAQ disclosures remain usable without JavaScript.

## Proof Discipline

Do not imply diagnosis, coverage, response time, licensing, customer outcomes,
or measured performance. The page may explain what a technician would inspect;
it may not claim what a real property needs without evidence.

## Routing Boundary

Route here for private wells, pumps, pressure tanks, filtration, rural water,
water treatment, symptom-first field service, equipment explainers, and
property-specific request flows. It can donate diagnostic and proof-safe
patterns to adjacent trades, but it should not become a generic plumbing,
electrical, pest-control, SaaS, or agency template.
