Our Story

Smooth is a discipline, not a shortcut.

Marisol Vega trained in traditional sugaring technique before Atlanta had a single studio built around it.

She came home from that training with one belief: hair removal didn't have to feel transactional. In 2019 she opened a single treatment room in Midtown, mixing paste from sugar, lemon, and water in a kitchen the size of a closet. No synthetic resins, no strips, no fragrance layered over discomfort — just a warm paste worked in the direction hair actually grows.

That belief became a studio, then five. The kitchen is bigger now, but the paste recipe hasn't changed, and neither has the pace: every appointment still opens with a real skin consult, not a rush to the table.

Sugaring Prep

Ready your skin, either direction.

  • Let hair reach ¼ inch — about two weeks of growth gives the paste something to grip.

  • Exfoliate 24 hours before — never the same day, which can leave skin too reactive.

  • Pause retinoids and acids for 48 hours — thinner skin barriers don't sugar comfortably.

  • Skip tanning or sun exposure on the treatment area for 24 hours prior.

  • Arrive fragrance-free where you can — bare skin reads best for your esthetician.

  • Avoid heat and heavy sweat for 24 hours — that means saunas, hot yoga, and long hot showers.

  • Wear loose, breathable fabric over treated skin for the rest of the day.

  • Hold off on retinoids and acids for 48 hours to let the skin barrier settle.

  • Begin gentle exfoliation on day three to help prevent ingrown hairs.

  • Moisturize daily — fragrance-free lotion is kindest on freshly treated skin.

New to sugaring, or reactive skin? Mention it at booking — a free patch test is standard for every first-time guest.
