The Velvet Fig method

Smooth is a discipline, not a shortcut.

Marisol Vega trained in traditional sugaring technique before Atlanta had a single studio built around it.

In 2019 she opened one treatment room in Midtown, mixing paste from sugar, lemon, and water in a kitchen the size of a closet. No synthetic resins, no strips, no fragrance layered over discomfort: just warm paste worked in the direction hair naturally grows.

That belief became five studios. The kitchen is bigger now, but the recipe and pace have not changed. Every appointment still opens with a skin consult, not a rush to the table.

“Sugaring isn't a faster wax. It's a gentler relationship with your own skin.”

Marisol Vega, founder

How a visit unfolds

Four steps, zero rush.

Every appointment follows the same unhurried shape, from first patch test to regular maintenance.

  1. 01

    Skin consult

    Your esthetician reviews sensitivities, skin history, and what today's visit should feel like.

  2. 02

    Warm paste

    Skin is cleansed, dried, and lightly powdered while the paste rests at body temperature.

  3. 03

    Directional technique

    Paste is applied against growth and lifted with growth to reduce pulling on live skin.

  4. 04

    Cool-down

    A soothing balm and printed aftercare guidance close the appointment.

Sugaring prep

Ready your skin, either direction.

Simple timing matters more than a complicated routine. First-time guests and reactive skin can request a free patch test.

  • Let hair reach ¼ inch. About two weeks of growth gives the paste something to grip.
  • Exfoliate 24 hours before. Avoid exfoliating on the same day.
  • Pause retinoids and acids for 48 hours. Thinner skin barriers do not sugar comfortably.
  • Skip tanning for 24 hours. Arrive fragrance-free where possible.
  • Avoid heat and heavy sweat for 24 hours. That includes saunas, hot yoga, and long hot showers.
  • Wear loose, breathable fabric. Give treated skin room to settle.
  • Begin gentle exfoliation on day three. Moisturize daily with fragrance-free lotion.
  • Hold retinoids and acids for 48 hours. Let the skin barrier recover first.

Questions

Answered plainly, before you ask twice.

Prefer to talk it through? Call 404.555.0166 during studio hours.

Does sugaring hurt more than waxing?

Most guests describe it as noticeably gentler. Paste is removed in the direction hair grows, which creates less pulling on the follicle than a wax strip applied against growth.

How long should hair be before my appointment?

About ¼ inch: roughly two weeks of growth after your last shave or four weeks after a wax.

Is sugaring suitable for sensitive or reactive skin?

The paste does not bond to live skin cells the way some wax formulas can. Velvet Fig recommends a free patch test for rosacea, eczema, reactive skin, or first-time guests.

What if this is my first time?

Mention it when you book. Every first-time guest receives a short patch test and a slower walkthrough of each step.

What is the cancellation policy?

Cancellation or rescheduling is free up to 24 hours before your visit. Inside 24 hours, a short-notice fee applies.

Is the paste really all-natural?

Three ingredients: sugar, lemon, and water. It is mixed at the Midtown kitchen and distributed to all five studios.
