# Holland Grading - Route Prompt

Build a terrain-first land-clearing, grading, excavation, drainage, demolition,
or site-preparation website. It should feel like a professional field operation
that understands access, grade, water, equipment, and handoff, not a generic
contractor template.

Use deep charcoal, moss, clay, weathered neutrals, water blue, and one
safety-signal yellow. The first viewport should be an immersive mountain
sitework scene with detailed machinery, earth layers, contour lines, and a
compact grade board. Inline SVG must carry realistic mechanical and topographic
detail; do not use cartoon equipment.

Build the complete flow:

1. Sticky navigation and one site-walk action.
2. Full-bleed terrain and equipment hero with grade-section board.
3. Accessible before-and-after grading comparison.
4. Service-phase tabs for clearing, grade and access, drainage, and closeout.
5. Site-walk-to-closeout process timeline.
6. Detailed dozer, excavator, skid-steer, and haul-truck studies.
7. Property-specific coverage checker with a conceptual topographic plate.
8. Filterable proof-safe field studies.
9. Site-walk brief planner that builds a useful checklist.
10. FAQ and a validated site-walk request with success and reset states.

Interactions must explain the work: range and pointer comparison, roving-focus
tabs, honest coverage-review state, field-study filtering, checklist assembly,
specific form validation, mobile menu focus control, and reduced-motion
fallbacks.

Use system fonts, semantic HTML, CSS, detailed inline SVG, and minimal vanilla
JavaScript. Do not use external assets, stock machinery, generic service-card
rows, emoji icons, fake project photos, or low-detail construction symbols.

Proof policy: invent no licenses, insurance, permits, service territory, project
photos, testimonials, fleet counts, prices, estimates, or timing. Coverage,
operating base, and business details must be verified before publishing.
