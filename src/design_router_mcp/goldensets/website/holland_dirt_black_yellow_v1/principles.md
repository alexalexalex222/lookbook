# Holland Grading - Design Principles

This is the sitework and earthmoving anchor. It earns trust by making terrain,
sequence, and equipment legible.

## Composition

- The first viewport is an unframed terrain story with one direct site-walk
  action. It is not a centered headline floating over an empty backdrop.
- A compact grade board turns the hero from atmosphere into an instrument.
- The page sequence follows the work: existing condition, service phase,
  process, machinery, coverage, field studies, planning, request.
- Detailed machinery and sectional drawings use multiple materials, joints,
  hoses, tracks, edges, wear, and shadows. Simple equipment icons are not enough.
- Section headings stay left-led. Centering is reserved for compact controls and
  success states.

## Visual System

- Neutral ramp: paper, weathered stone, graphite, deep charcoal.
- Terrain ramp: clay and earth with moss for retained landscape.
- Safety yellow marks action, measurement, and machine identity; it does not
  flood every surface.
- Water blue is reserved for drainage and flow.
- Cards are hard-edged field plates with restrained elevation.

## Interaction

- The before-and-after control exposes meaningful site change and works by
  keyboard and pointer.
- Service tabs use roving focus and preserve phase context.
- Coverage returns a review state until a real service boundary is supplied.
- The planner creates a useful site-walk checklist rather than a fake estimate.
- Forms preserve input, expose specific errors, and state that the static donor
  did not transmit data.

## Proof Discipline

Trust comes from scope clarity, process, detailed equipment literacy, and
proof-safe studies. Never replace missing projects with fake photos, invented
territories, fleet counts, badges, ratings, pricing, or completion timelines.

## Routing Boundary

Route here for land clearing, grading, excavation, site prep, earthwork,
drainage, driveways, ponds, demolition, steep terrain, and equipment-led local
service sites. Do not route here for generic home services that lack terrain,
heavy equipment, or field-sequencing needs.
