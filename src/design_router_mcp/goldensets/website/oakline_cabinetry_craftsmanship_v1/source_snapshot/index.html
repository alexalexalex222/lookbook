The Oakline Standard

Built Different, By Design

Cabinetry is graded on what's behind the door. Here's what we build with, compared to what most "custom" shops start from.

Comparison of Oakline construction methods against typical big-box custom cabinetry
Build element The Oakline Standard Typical Big-Box "Custom"
Box construction 3/4" maple plywood, dado-and-glue assembly 1/2" particleboard, stapled and glued
Drawer joinery Hand-fit through-dovetail, solid maple sides Doweled or stapled butt joints
Face frame Solid hardwood, mortise-and-tenon Printed laminate over particleboard
Hardware Soft-close hinges & slides, lifetime mechanism warranty Standard friction hinges, 1-year warranty
Finish process Hand-rubbed, multi-coat conversion varnish Single-pass sprayed lacquer
Shop drawings Room-measured shop drawings before a board is cut Stock modular sizes, trimmed on-site
Drawer glides Full-extension, 100-lb rated undermount glides Side-mount glides, partial extension
Warranty 5-year structural, lifetime on hardware mechanisms 1-year limited, hardware excluded
Who's Building It

A Shop of Eleven, Not a Franchise of Thousands

Oakline started in a two-car garage with a single table saw and a waiting list built entirely on referrals. Eighteen years later we're still a small shop on purpose — eleven woodworkers, one in-house designer, and a policy against subcontracting the work we put our name on.

If your project is on our floor, one of us is the one who built it. That's the whole model.

  • We draw before we cut. Every project gets a full shop-drawing approval before any material is touched.
  • We say no on purpose. We take on what our current schedule can build well, not everything that calls.
  • We fix what we build. If a drawer sticks in year three, the person who built it is who shows up.
