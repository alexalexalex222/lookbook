The Oakline standard

The detail is not decoration. It is how the cabinet holds together.

Premium cabinetry should remain convincing with the door open. This study follows the load from the face frame through the box, drawer, hinge, and wall connection.

  1. 01

    Inset door, held in a true frame

    Solid hardwood stiles and rails surround a floating center panel. The reveal stays deliberately narrow and even, so misalignment has nowhere to hide.

  2. 02

    Grain follows the part

    Stiles run vertically, rails run horizontally, and adjacent fronts are laid out together before cutting. The figure reads continuously instead of changing direction at random.

  3. 03

    Dovetails carry the drawer

    Solid maple drawer sides interlock at the corners. The joint is structural first; the fact that it remains visible is useful accountability.

  4. 04

    Hardware is fitted, not merely attached

    Hinges and undermount slides are positioned for the actual door and drawer weight, then tuned again after installation.

Four stages, one shop

The room is measured at the beginning and tuned again at the end.

No separate box supplier and no handoff to an unknown installer. The same drawing set follows the project through the shop and back into the room.

Start with the room
  1. 01

    Week 1 · On site

    Design consultation

    We walk the room, learn how it is used, and record walls, openings, appliances, and the small inconsistencies that stock sizes ignore.

  2. 02

    Weeks 2–3 · Drawing table

    Shop drawings and samples

    Proportions, reveals, door construction, finish samples, and hardware are resolved in one drawing set before material is ordered.

  3. 03

    Weeks 4–8 · Shop floor

    Build, fit, and finish

    Boxes, frames, doors, and drawers are built and pre-fit together. Grain is laid out as a complete elevation, then the finish is built in controlled coats.

  4. 04

    Week 9 · In the room

    Install and walkthrough

    The installation is scribed to the house, hardware is tuned under real load, and the final walkthrough happens before the project is called complete.

Questions we hear often

What changes when the work is truly custom?

The design consultation is where project-specific answers begin. These are the questions that shape most first conversations.

How long does a full kitchen project take?

Most kitchens run 8 to 10 weeks from signed drawings to final walkthrough. Bathroom vanities and built-ins are usually faster.

Do you build in-house or install pre-made boxes?

Every box, door, and drawer is built in the Columbus shop. Oakline does not resell modular cabinet lines.

Which wood species and finishes are available?

White oak, walnut, soft maple, and rift-sawn oak are the most requested species, alongside studio-mixed paint colors.

What does full-custom mean here?

The design starts from the room's actual measurements rather than a catalog of standard box sizes. Proportions and fillers are resolved in the drawing, not improvised during installation.

Do you coordinate counters, electrical, and plumbing?

Oakline handles cabinetry, installation, and finish carpentry, and coordinates directly with the other trades so work happens in the correct sequence.
